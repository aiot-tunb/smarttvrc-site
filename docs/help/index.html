🚀 Quick Start 5 min ▼
Same Wi-Fi network
Make sure your phone and TV are connected to the same Wi-Fi router. This is required for all Wi-Fi control features.
Enable network remote on your TV
Go to TV Settings → see the brand-specific section below for exact steps.
Open the app → tap + to add TV
The app will auto-scan your network. Your TV should appear within 10–20 seconds. Tap it to start pairing.
Accept the pairing request on your TV
A PIN or accept dialog will appear on your TV screen. Enter the PIN in the app, or press OK on your TV remote to accept.
Start controlling!
You're ready. The remote screen will open automatically after pairing.
Tap the TV card on the Home screen any time to reconnect instantly.
📺 Add TV Screen ▼
Auto Scan tab
Tap Scan to discover all TVs on your network automatically. Supported brands appear as cards — tap one to start pairing.
Manual tab
If auto-scan doesn't find your TV, switch to the Manual tab and enter the TV's IP address directly. You can find the IP in your TV's network settings or router admin page.
TV not showing up?
Make sure remote control is enabled in your TV settings. See the Fix Connection section for detailed steps per brand.
Scan takes 10–30 seconds depending on network size. Keep the screen open until it finishes.
🔗 Connection Guide by Brand ▼
LG WebOS
Settings → All Settings → General → Mobile TV On → Turn On LG Connect Apps.

When the app connects for the first time, a pairing popup appears on the TV — press Allow.
Samsung Tizen
Settings → General → External Device Manager → Device Connect Manager → Access Notification → Always Allow (or First Time Only).

A PIN or accept dialog appears at first pairing.
Android TV / Google TV
No special setting needed — Android TV Remote Service runs automatically.

On first connect, a 6-digit PIN appears on screen. Enter it in the app.
Panasonic Viera
Modern models (Android TV): Same as Android TV above.

Older Viera models: Network Settings → Network TV Manufacturer's Settings → TV Remote App Settings → On.
Roku
Settings → System → Advanced System Settings → External Control → ECP and DIAL → Enabled.

No pairing needed — Roku uses open ECP protocol.
Some TVs require the app to be on your TV's allowed device list. If the TV keeps rejecting connections, check the trusted devices list in TV settings.
⌨️ Bluetooth Keyboard & Mouse Android ▼
This feature turns your Android phone into a Bluetooth HID keyboard and trackpad — useful for typing passwords, URLs, or controlling a TV that doesn't support Wi-Fi control. Requires Android 9 (Pie) or higher.

Pair your TV via Bluetooth first
Go to your phone's Bluetooth settings and pair with your TV (the TV must appear as a Bluetooth device). The TV typically shows up as its model name.
Open BT Keyboard & Mouse in the app
From the Remote screen, tap the ⌨ icon in the top bar. Or tap the keyboard icon on the Home screen.
Select your TV from the list
The screen shows all paired Bluetooth devices. Tap your TV to connect. You can also tap Scan to discover nearby devices.
Use the Trackpad and Keyboard tabs
Trackpad: drag to move cursor, two-finger scroll available. Use arrow buttons for D-pad navigation.
Keyboard: type text directly, use shift and special keys. Tap Send or Enter to confirm.
Not available on iOS — Apple restricts Bluetooth HID peripheral mode on iPhone. Use the Wi-Fi remote instead.
🔧 Fix Connection Problems ▼
Check same network
Phone and TV must be on the same Wi-Fi network (same SSID). Guest networks and VPNs can isolate devices from each other.
Enable remote control on TV
Each brand has a different setting path. See the By Brand section above for exact steps. The option is often called "Remote Device", "Mobile TV On", or "External Control".
Restart TV and try again
Power cycle the TV (fully off, wait 10 seconds, turn back on). Then try scanning again in the app.
Check TV firewall / AP Isolation
Some routers have "AP Isolation" or "Client Isolation" that prevents devices on the same network from talking to each other. Disable it in your router settings.
Remove and re-add the TV
On the Home screen, long-press the TV card → Remove. Then add it again. This clears stale credentials.
Grant Bluetooth permission (BT Keyboard)
Go to phone Settings → Apps → SmartTV RC → Permissions → Nearby Devices → Allow.
If the TV was found before but now shows offline, tap the TV card to try reconnecting — it often reconnects in 2–3 seconds.
